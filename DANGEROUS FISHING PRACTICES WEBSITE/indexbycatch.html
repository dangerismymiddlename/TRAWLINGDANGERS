Unintentionally caught fish or marine organisms that are not the target species when fishing. Ecologically, bycatch can change the availability of prey, which affects marine ecosystems and the productivity of fisheries. Bycatch can negatively affect species such as dolphins, sea turtles, protected fish, and whales by harming animals, contributing to population declines, and delaying / prevent population recovery.
